## Tian_Yi_Gui_Ren_noble_star
tags: Jia, Wu, Gui, Ren, Xin, noble_star, heavenly_stem, earthly_branch, luck
school: classical
confidence: high

Tian Yi Gui Ren (天乙贵人, Heavenly Noble Star) is one of the most auspicious stars in BaZi. Each Day Master has two specific branches that activate it. Jia and Wu Day Masters: Chou and Wei. Yi and Ji: Zi and Shen. Bing and Ding: Hai and You. Geng and Gui: Chou and Wei. Xin: Wu and Yin. Ren: Mao and Si. When a noble branch appears in the natal chart or activates through a luck/annual cycle, it indicates timely help from influential benefactors, protection in crises, and smooth resolution of difficult situations.

## Tian_De_Gui_Ren_heavenly_virtue
tags: noble_star, month, earthly_branch, luck, protection
school: classical
confidence: high

Tian De Gui Ren (天德贵人, Heavenly Virtue Noble) is determined by birth month. Month 1 (Yin): Ding. Month 2 (Mao): Shen. Month 3 (Chen): Ren. Month 4 (Si): Xin. Month 5 (Wu): Hai. Month 6 (Wei): Jia. Month 7 (Shen): Gui. Month 8 (You): Yin. Month 9 (Xu): Bing. Month 10 (Hai): Yi. Month 11 (Zi): Wu. Month 12 (Chou): Geng. When the corresponding stem appears elsewhere in the chart, it brings protection from disasters, reduced severity of punishments and clashes, and noble assistance throughout life.

## Yue_De_Gui_Ren_monthly_virtue
tags: noble_star, month, earthly_branch, luck, protection
school: classical
confidence: high

Yue De Gui Ren (月德贵人, Monthly Virtue Noble) also determined by birth month. Yin, Wu, Xu months: Bing. Si, You, Chou months: Geng. Shen, Zi, Chen months: Ren. Hai, Mao, Wei months: Jia. When the corresponding stem appears in another pillar, it reduces the severity of clashes and punishments in that pillar and invites higher-quality benefactor energy. Yue De is considered slightly less powerful than Tian De but more accessible, as it recurs quarterly.

## Lu_Shen_personal_nobleman
tags: heavenly_stem, earthly_branch, luck, career, resource, companion
school: classical
confidence: high

Lu Shen (禄神, Personal Nobleman / Officer Star) is the branch corresponding directly to the Day Master's stem: Jia→Yin, Yi→Mao, Bing→Si, Ding→Wu, Wu→Si, Ji→Wu, Geng→Shen, Xin→You, Ren→Hai, Gui→Zi. When Lu appears in the natal branches (especially month or day), it indicates strong career support and self-sufficiency throughout life. A Lu in the luck cycle brings career opportunities and income growth. A clashed Lu year can signal career disruptions or loss of status.

## Wen_Chang_literary_star
tags: output, creativity, education, heavenly_stem, earthly_branch, luck
school: classical
confidence: high

Wen Chang (文昌, Literary Star) is associated with intellectual brilliance, academic achievement, and writing talent. Each Day Master has one corresponding branch: Jia→Si, Yi→Wu, Bing→Shen, Ding→You, Wu→Shen, Ji→You, Geng→Hai, Xin→Zi, Ren→Yin, Gui→Mao. A natal Wen Chang supports careers in academia, literature, law, teaching, or any field requiring intellectual output. Wen Chang in the luck cycle brings study success, examinations passed, and publications. It combines well with the Eating God star.

## Jin_Yu_Tang_gold_jade_hall
tags: wealth, noble_star, heavenly_stem, earthly_branch, luck
school: classical
confidence: medium

Jin Yu Tang (金玉堂, Gold and Jade Hall) is a less commonly cited auspicious star indicating wealth accumulation and social elevation. It appears when specific stem-branch combinations align in the natal chart or luck cycle. Associated with Jia or Yi Day Masters finding Xu or Chou in their luck pillars, or Geng/Xin Day Masters encountering Chen or Chou. Indicates periods of material prosperity, real estate gains, and elevation in social standing.

## Fu_Xing_fortune_star
tags: luck, noble_star, heavenly_stem, year, month
school: classical
confidence: medium

Fu Xing (福星, Fortune Star) is determined by the year branch and indicates where luck flows in a given year. It represents unexpected blessings, smooth relationships, and reduced obstacles. When Fu Xing aligns with the Day Master's favorable element in an annual pillar, the year carries a distinctive quality of ease and positive coincidences. Less structural than Gui Ren stars but adds a layer of "life feels supported" quality to chart interpretation.

## Tian_Yi_activation_by_clash
tags: noble_star, clash, earthly_branch, luck_cycle, year
school: classical
confidence: medium

The Tian Yi Gui Ren noble branches can be activated not only by direct presence but also by being clashed or combined in a luck or annual pillar. Some schools argue that a clash "opens" the noble star, releasing its benefactor energy in a burst — often manifesting as an unexpected rescue or turning point. This interpretation is more common in Dong Gong and date-selection schools. Classical texts note that a clashed noble in the natal chart can indicate an unreliable or distant benefactor relationship.

## personal_auspicious_hours_by_daymaster
tags: heavenly_stem, earthly_branch, hour, luck, noble_star
school: dong_gong
confidence: medium

In date selection and daily planning, each Day Master has auspicious hours tied to their noble star branches. For Jia/Wu Day Masters: Chou (1-3am) and Wei (1-3pm) hours are most auspicious for important activities. For Yi/Ji: Zi (11pm-1am) and Shen (3-5pm). For Bing/Ding: Hai (9-11pm) and You (5-7pm). For Geng/Gui: Chou and Wei. For Xin: Wu (11am-1pm) and Yin (3-5am). For Ren: Mao (5-7am) and Si (9-11am). Using one's noble hour for signing contracts, starting projects, or meeting important people amplifies auspicious outcomes.
