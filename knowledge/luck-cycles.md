## luck_cycle_dominant_influence
tags: luck_cycle, daymaster, strong, weak, resource, output, wealth, power
school: classical
confidence: high

A 10-year luck cycle represents the dominant environmental influence overlaid on the natal chart. A favorable cycle can unlock hidden potential in a good chart or salvage a difficult one; an unfavorable cycle suppresses even the strongest charts. The cycle's heavenly stem engages primarily with natal stems (combining, clashing, generating), while the earthly branch interacts with natal branches (opening storage houses, activating punishments or combinations). Understanding which luck cycle a person is in is essential before making any chart-based predictions.

## luck_cycle_stem_interactions
tags: luck_cycle, heavenly_stem, combine, clash, daymaster, natal
school: classical
confidence: high

The heavenly stem of the luck cycle engages primarily with natal heavenly stems. A combining stem can transform a natal star's function entirely — a natal Wealth star that combines with the luck cycle stem may become inaccessible (combined away) during that decade. A clashing stem creates disruption in the domain represented by the natal star being clashed. The stem interaction provides the "backdrop energy" of the decade — the ongoing energetic theme that colors all events within that 10-year period.

## luck_cycle_branch_interactions
tags: luck_cycle, earthly_branch, combine, clash, storage, hidden_stem, natal
school: classical
confidence: high

The earthly branch of the luck cycle interacts with natal branches, opening or closing storage houses that release hidden stems. When a storage branch (Chen, Wei, Xu, Chou) in the natal chart is clashed by the luck cycle branch, it "opens" and releases its hidden stems — triggering the events represented by those stems. This layer typically produces concrete, observable life events rather than the abstract thematic energy of the stem. The first five years of a cycle are often dominated by the stem; the second five by the branch.

## annual_pillar_as_catalyst
tags: luck_cycle, year, earthly_branch, heavenly_stem, combine, clash
school: classical
confidence: high

The yearly pillar acts as a trigger mechanism within the luck cycle context. Events predicted by the luck cycle framework typically manifest concretely when the annual pillar's stem or branch combines or clashes with natal or luck cycle elements simultaneously. A favorable luck cycle with an opposing annual pillar creates a temporary setback within a generally good decade. Conversely, a challenging luck cycle with a supportive annual year creates a brief window of opportunity within a difficult period. Always read luck cycle and annual pillar together.

## favorable_luck_cycle_assessment
tags: luck_cycle, daymaster, strong, weak, resource, output, wealth, power
school: classical
confidence: high

A luck cycle is favorable when it provides what the Day Master needs: weak charts benefit from Resource and Companion cycles; strong charts benefit from Output, Wealth, and Authority cycles. The most productive decades are those where the cycle's elements serve as the "useful god" (用神) of the natal chart. A luck cycle with the exact useful god element — especially when rooted in the branch — produces the highest quality outcomes in career, relationships, and health simultaneously.

## hour_pillar_late_life_activation
tags: luck_cycle, hour, earthly_branch, heavenly_stem, later_life
school: classical
confidence: high

The hour pillar governs later life (after roughly age 50) and descendants. It activates more fully under luck and annual cycle influence as the person ages. Charts with a strong, well-aspected hour pillar often improve dramatically in the final two to three luck cycles. An unknown birth hour creates an incomplete chart — the missing fourth pillar limits prediction accuracy for later life and offspring outcomes. When the hour is unknown, analysts focus more heavily on the three known pillars and treat late-life predictions as ranges.
