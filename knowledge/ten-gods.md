## Bi_Jian_companion
tags: companion, daymaster, strong, weak, heavenly_stem, element
school: classical
confidence: high

Bi Jian (比肩, Direct Companion) shares the same element and polarity as the Day Master. It provides self-confidence, independence, and peer support. In a weak chart it strengthens the Day Master; in an already strong chart it creates rivalry, stubbornness, and competition over wealth. Excessive Bi Jian depletes Direct Wealth and indicates financial instability or conflicts with siblings and partners.

## Jie_Cai_rob_wealth
tags: companion, daymaster, strong, weak, wealth, heavenly_stem, element
school: classical
confidence: high

Jie Cai (劫财, Rob Wealth / Indirect Companion) shares the same element but opposite polarity to the Day Master. It represents competitive energy that aggressively seeks resources. Useful in weak charts for support, but in strong or overloaded charts it signals financial loss, betrayal by partners, and impulsive spending. Jie Cai years or cycles frequently bring unexpected expenses or investments gone wrong.

## Shi_Shen_eating_god
tags: output, food_god, creativity, daymaster, heavenly_stem, element
school: classical
confidence: high

Shi Shen (食神, Eating God) is the indirect output star — same polarity as the Day Master. It represents creativity, artistic talent, gastronomy, leisurely expression, and indirect income. Shi Shen controls the Seven Killings star, offering protection. A chart with a well-placed Eating God supports talent in arts, food, or entertainment industries. Excessive Shi Shen can indicate scattered energy or over-indulgence.

---

## Shang_Guan_hurting_officer
tags: output, authority, creativity, daymaster, heavenly_stem, element, power
school: classical
confidence: high

Shang Guan (伤官, Hurting Officer) is the direct output star — opposite polarity to the Day Master. It is the most forceful creative and expressive star, associated with brilliance, artistic mastery, rebellion, and non-conformity. Shang Guan clashes with Direct Officer (Zheng Guan), undermining career authority and rules. In female charts it can signal relationship tensions with authoritative partners. When properly channeled it brings exceptional talent and charisma.

## Pian_Cai_indirect_wealth
tags: wealth, father, daymaster, heavenly_stem, element, resource
school: classical
confidence: high

Pian Cai (偏财, Indirect Wealth) is wealth of the opposite polarity from the Day Master — it represents windfall income, speculative gains, business profits, and the father. Geng and Xin Day Masters with strong Pian Cai in favorable cycles may excel in trading, sales, or investment. Pian Cai also governs the emotional relationship with the father; a clashed or harmed Pian Cai can indicate a distant or troubled paternal relationship.

## Zheng_Cai_direct_wealth
tags: wealth, spouse, daymaster, heavenly_stem, element, resource
school: classical
confidence: high

Zheng Cai (正财, Direct Wealth) is wealth of the same polarity as the Day Master — it represents stable income, property, diligent earning, and in male charts the wife or primary partner. A well-rooted Zheng Cai in the chart supports consistent financial flow. If the Day Master is too weak to hold wealth, Zheng Cai becomes a burden. Direct Wealth combined with strong Output stars creates the classic wealth-generating chart structure.

## Qi_Sha_seven_killings
tags: power, authority, discipline, daymaster, heavenly_stem, element, strong
school: classical
confidence: high

Qi Sha (七杀, Seven Killings) is the indirect power star — it controls the Day Master with force. A raw Seven Killings without control (Eating God or Direct Resource) creates aggression, risk-taking, and authority conflicts. When controlled and properly channeled, it becomes the driving force behind military leaders, surgeons, and high-achieving executives. Female charts with strong Qi Sha may face dominant or controlling partners. Qi Sha years bring pressure, challenges, and forced growth.

## Zheng_Guan_direct_officer
tags: power, authority, career, spouse, daymaster, heavenly_stem, element
school: classical
confidence: high

Zheng Guan (正官, Direct Officer) is the direct power star — it governs with rules and propriety. It represents career authority, social status, promotions, law, and in female charts the husband or primary partner. A single, well-placed Direct Officer in a chart is highly favorable. Multiple Direct Officers suggest career instability or authority conflicts. Direct Officer years and cycles typically bring promotions, recognition, and institutional support when the Day Master is strong enough to receive it.

## Pian_Yin_indirect_resource
tags: resource, seal, mentor, daymaster, heavenly_stem, element, weak
school: classical
confidence: high

Pian Yin (偏印, Indirect Resource / Seal) provides unconventional nourishment and protection to the Day Master. It supports through alternative means — mentors from unusual fields, self-taught knowledge, healing arts, spirituality, or esoteric study. Pian Yin can suppress the Eating God (Shi Shen), creating what classical texts call the "Eating God meets Seal" pattern — artistic talent suppressed by over-analysis or parental interference. In weak charts it is valuable; in excess it creates isolation.

## ten_gods_favorable_unfavorable_chart
tags: daymaster, strong, weak, resource, output, wealth, power, companion
school: classical
confidence: high

The usefulness of each Ten God depends entirely on Day Master strength. A weak Day Master benefits from Resource (both Yin types) and Companion (both Bi types) stars, and is burdened by Wealth, Output, and Power stars that drain it. A strong Day Master benefits from Output, Wealth, and Power stars that channel its excess, and is further burdened by additional Resource or Companion stars. Misidentifying strength is the most common error in BaZi analysis.

## output_stars_creative_expression
tags: output, food_god, creativity, wealth, heavenly_stem, element
school: classical
confidence: high

The two Output stars (Shi Shen and Shang Guan) represent the Day Master's productive expression in the world. They generate Wealth (both types) and are considered the "bridge" between the Day Master's inner potential and outer material success. A chart with strong Output and accessible Wealth creates a productive wealth-generating structure. Output stars also govern children — their quantity, quality, and timing in a chart.

---

## power_stars_control_discipline
tags: power, authority, discipline, career, heavenly_stem, element
school: classical
confidence: high

The two Power stars (Qi Sha and Zheng Guan) represent external authority and discipline acting upon the Day Master. When the Day Master has sufficient strength and the Power star is properly controlled, it channels ambition into achievement. When Power dominates a weak Day Master without Resource support, it creates pressure without reward — manifesting as chronic stress, health issues, or institutional oppression. The relationship between Power and Resource stars determines career trajectory.
