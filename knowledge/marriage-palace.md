## marriage_palace_day_branch
tags: marriage, spouse, relationship, earthly_branch, day, palace
school: classical
confidence: high

The Day Branch (日支, Day Earthly Branch) is the primary marriage palace in BaZi. It represents the intimate relationship zone, the quality of the spousal relationship, and the energetic foundation of the home. A Day Branch that is strong, rooted, and unfettered (not clashed, punished, or harmed) indicates a stable and supportive primary relationship. Frequent clashes or punishments to the Day Branch across luck cycles and annual pillars correlate with relationship disruptions.

## spouse_star_male_chart
tags: marriage, spouse, wealth, male, relationship, heavenly_stem, earthly_branch
school: classical
confidence: high

In a male chart, the wife is represented primarily by the Wealth star (Direct Wealth for the primary partner, Indirect Wealth for secondary or unconventional relationships). The strength, rootedness, and condition of the Wealth star determines the quality of the spousal relationship. A Wealth star rooted in the Day Branch creates strong partnership energy. A Wealth star that is absent from the chart or heavily clashed may indicate delayed marriage, difficulty sustaining relationships, or a partner who is absent or challenging.

## spouse_star_female_chart
tags: marriage, spouse, power, female, relationship, heavenly_stem, earthly_branch
school: classical
confidence: high

In a female chart, the husband is represented primarily by the Power star (Direct Officer for conventional partnerships, Seven Killings for unconventional or forceful partners). A single, well-placed Direct Officer in the chart generally indicates a reliable and conventional partner. Multiple Power stars suggest difficulty with committed relationships or serial partnerships. Seven Killings as the primary spouse star correlates with a dominant, strong-willed, or difficult partner — but also with a highly driven and successful one.

## marriage_palace_clash_effects
tags: marriage, spouse, clash, earthly_branch, day, luck_cycle, year
school: classical
confidence: high

When the Day Branch (marriage palace) is clashed by a luck cycle or annual pillar branch, it activates the marriage zone and forces relationship events — both positive (meeting a partner, marriage) and negative (separation, divorce). The nature of the outcome depends on whether the activated elements are favorable or unfavorable to the Day Master. A clash to a weak Day Branch in an already strained chart predicts separation. A clash to a stagnant but healthy Day Branch can trigger meeting a partner or a relationship breakthrough.

## marriage_palace_combinations
tags: marriage, spouse, combine, earthly_branch, day, luck_cycle, year
school: classical
confidence: high

When the Day Branch combines with a luck cycle or annual branch to form a six-combination (六合), the marriage palace is activated through attraction rather than force. This is often the most favorable timing for meeting a spouse or deepening a committed relationship. The Tiger-Pig combination, Rat-Ox combination, or Rabbit-Dog combination activating the Day Branch in a compatible year creates a window of high romantic probability. However, if the combined transformation produces an unfavorable element for the Day Master, the relationship brings complications alongside attraction.

## early_late_marriage_indicators
tags: marriage, spouse, earthly_branch, luck_cycle, year, weak, strong
school: classical
confidence: medium

Early marriage (before 25) is indicated when the spouse star is prominent in the natal chart (especially in the month or day pillar) and a luck cycle supporting that star activates in the teens or early twenties. Late marriage (after 35) is suggested when the spouse star is absent, excessively clashed, or only appears in the annual pillar during later luck cycles. A chart where the marriage palace (Day Branch) is involved in a three-punishment or self-punishment pattern often experiences significant delays or complications in establishing stable partnership.

## hua_gai_lone_star_celibacy
tags: marriage, spouse, earthly_branch, luck, spiritual, Hua_Gai
school: classical
confidence: medium

Hua Gai (华盖, Artistic Canopy / Lone Star) is a branch star indicating artistic talent but also isolation and a tendency toward spiritual or solitary pursuits. For Shen-Zi-Chen frame: Chen is Hua Gai. For Yin-Wu-Xu frame: Xu. For Si-You-Chou frame: Chou. For Hai-Mao-Wei frame: Wei. A natal Hua Gai in the Day Branch often correlates with delayed marriage, a preference for solitude, or deep artistic and spiritual inclinations. Multiple Hua Gai in the chart strongly suggests a calling toward monasticism, spiritual work, or lifelong creative independence over conventional partnership.

## spouse_palace_hidden_stem_quality
tags: marriage, spouse, earthly_branch, hidden_stem, day, resource, wealth, power
school: classical
confidence: high

The hidden stems within the Day Branch reveal the character and role of the spouse. If the hidden stem of the Day Branch is the spouse star (Wealth for males, Power for females), the partner embodies those qualities strongly. If the hidden stem is a Resource star, the partner takes on a nurturing or mothering role. If it is an Output star, the partner is creative and expressive. A clash or combination that changes the effective hidden stem can symbolize a partner who transforms significantly in character over the course of the marriage.
