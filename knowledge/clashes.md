## Zi_Wu_clash_water_fire
tags: Zi, Wu, clash, earthly_branch, water, fire, relationship, health
school: classical
confidence: high

Zi (Rat) and Wu (Horse) form a direct clash of Water against Fire. This is one of the most dynamic clashes, as it pits peak Water against peak Fire energy. When active in the natal chart, it creates constant internal tension between emotional depth (Water) and passionate expression (Fire). In luck and annual cycles, Zi-Wu clash destabilizes the palace it activates — relationship palace if the day branch is involved, career if the month branch. Health watch points: heart (Fire) and kidneys (Water). Events include relationship turbulence and sudden directional changes.

## Chou_Wei_clash_earth_storage
tags: Chou, Wei, clash, earthly_branch, Earth, wealth, storage
school: classical
confidence: high

Chou (Ox) and Wei (Goat) clash as two Earth storage branches containing conflicting hidden stems. Chou stores Metal and Water energy while Wei stores Fire and Wood — the clash releases both sets of hidden stems simultaneously, creating turbulence in financial storage and property matters. For charts with strong Wealth in Chou or Wei, this clash can scatter accumulated resources. In the month pillar, Chou-Wei clash often signals career instability and disrupted family foundations during the activated luck period.

## Yin_Shen_clash_travel_career
tags: Yin, Shen, clash, earthly_branch, Wood, Metal, travel, career
school: classical
confidence: high

Yin (Tiger) and Shen (Monkey) clash as Metal cuts Wood across the primary movement branches. Both contain Yi Ma (Travel Horse) energy, so this clash creates forced movement — sudden relocations, unexpected career changes, and disrupted travel plans. In the day pillar, Yin-Shen clash correlates with an unsettled relationship or a partner who triggers major life direction changes. Transportation accidents and orthopedic injuries are physical health risks to monitor during active years.

## Mao_You_clash_legal_documents
tags: Mao, You, clash, earthly_branch, Wood, Metal, legal, relationship
school: classical
confidence: high

Mao (Rabbit) and You (Rooster) clash as Metal dominates Wood in the pure elemental branches. These are the only two branches containing a single hidden stem each (Yi and Xin), making this clash exceptionally clean and direct. Legal disputes, documentation problems, and contract failures are common event types. In female charts, this clash to the day branch frequently correlates with relationship tension and control struggles. In male charts, it can indicate career setbacks from authority figures or contractual betrayals.

## Chen_Xu_clash_heavenly_storage
tags: Chen, Xu, clash, earthly_branch, Earth, psychology, hidden_stem
school: classical
confidence: high

Chen (Dragon) and Xu (Dog) clash as two Earth storage branches — called the "Heavenly Clash" in classical texts because both contain diverse hidden stems that are rapidly released. Chen holds Yi Wood, Wu Earth, and Gui Water; Xu holds Xin Metal, Ding Fire, and Wu Earth. The simultaneous release creates a complex energetic eruption. Psychological instability, sudden revelation of hidden matters, and the exposure of long-concealed situations are hallmarks of this clash. It is considered the most psychologically destabilizing of the six clashes.

## Si_Hai_clash_water_fire_intellectual
tags: Si, Hai, clash, earthly_branch, Fire, Water, intellect, contract
school: classical
confidence: high

Si (Snake) and Hai (Pig) clash as Water extinguishes Fire, disrupting intellectual and productive capacity. Si contains Bing Fire, Wu Earth, and Geng Metal; Hai contains Ren Water and Jia Wood. The clash releases these stems and disrupts financial and intellectual productivity. Contract failures, travel complications, and difficulties with financial planning are common manifestations. For Fire Day Masters, Si-Hai clash in a luck cycle can be particularly draining as it attacks the resource network around their self-element.
