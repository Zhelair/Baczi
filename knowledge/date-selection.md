## San_Sha_three_killings_direction
tags: San_Sha, year, earthly_branch, inauspicious, direction, clash
school: classical
confidence: high

San Sha (三煞, Three Killings) is a group of three branches that oppose the yearly ruling branch group. For years governed by Shen-Zi-Chen (Water frame): Si, Wu, Wei are Three Killings. For Yin-Wu-Xu (Fire frame): Hai, Zi, Chou. For Si-You-Chou (Metal frame): Yin, Mao, Chen. For Hai-Mao-Wei (Wood frame): Shen, You, Xu. The Three Killings direction must not be disturbed by construction, renovation, or major digging. Moving toward Three Killings in an important year increases risk of robbery, injury, and legal trouble.

## Sui_Po_year_breaker
tags: Sui_Po, year, earthly_branch, inauspicious, clash
school: classical
confidence: high

Sui Po (岁破, Year Breaker) is the branch directly clashing with the year branch. In a Zi year, Wu is Sui Po; in a Chou year, Wei is Sui Po, and so on. The Sui Po month and direction carry heightened risk of disruption, conflict with authority, and plans failing at critical moments. Avoid major decisions, surgeries, or travel departures on Sui Po days within the year. Marriages and business launches on Sui Po days often encounter unexpected reversals.

## Wu_Huang_five_yellow
tags: five_yellow, year, direction, inauspicious, construction
school: dong_gong
confidence: medium

Wu Huang (五黄, Five Yellow) is a feng shui star that moves to a different direction each year based on the flying star cycle. It is considered the most malevolent spatial energy and must not be disturbed by renovation, construction, or loud activity in its annual position. Disturbing Wu Huang can trigger illness, financial setbacks, and relationship breakdown for the occupants of that sector. The antidote is metal energy (wind chimes or metal objects) placed in that direction.

## Jian Chu daily_officer_system
tags: day, month, earthly_branch, inauspicious, auspicious, date_selection
school: dong_gong
confidence: high

The Jian Chu (建除) twelve-day officer cycle assigns a quality to each day based on the month and day branch relationship. Jian (Establish) and Man (Full) days support new beginnings; Ping (Balance) and Ding (Stable) are excellent for contracts and ceremonies. Sha (Destruction) and Po (Break) days must be avoided for important activities. Shou (Receive) days favor income and receiving. Kai (Open) days are ideal for grand openings and launches. This cycle is a foundational layer in Chinese date selection.

## inauspicious_red_days_conflict
tags: day, month, year, earthly_branch, heavenly_stem, inauspicious, clash
school: dong_gong
confidence: high

Days where the day stem-branch clashes with the month or year branch are considered red days for important activities. A day pillar clashing with the year branch activates the Annual Sha energy for that day. For example, in a Wu Xu year, days with Chen branch directly clash the year — these are dangerous days for surgery, marriage, or signing major contracts. The clash activates Sui Po energy, creating an unstable foundation for any event begun on that day.

## He_Ji_harmony_day
tags: day, month, earthly_branch, heavenly_stem, auspicious, combine, date_selection
school: dong_gong
confidence: high

He Ji (合吉, Harmony Auspicious) days occur when the day branch combines with the month branch to form a favorable elemental union. For example, in a Zi month (Water frame), a Si day creates a Si-Zi semi-combination. In date selection, harmony days create a cooperative energetic foundation ideal for weddings, business partnerships, and official signings. The transformed element of the combination should be favorable for the event type — Water for career, Wood for growth, Fire for visibility, Metal for precision.

## Yi_Ma_travel_horse
tags: Yi_Ma, year, earthly_branch, travel, movement, luck_cycle
school: classical
confidence: high

Yi Ma (驿马, Travel Horse) is the branch that drives movement in any given chart frame. For Shen-Zi-Chen frame: Yi Ma is Yin. For Yin-Wu-Xu frame: Shen. For Si-You-Chou frame: Hai. For Hai-Mao-Wei frame: Si. A natal Yi Ma indicates a life of frequent travel, relocation, or dynamic career movement. When Yi Ma activates in a luck or annual cycle, it brings opportunities abroad, career relocation, or significant change of environment. A clashed Yi Ma in a cycle forces unwanted movement or disrupted travel plans.

## Tao_Hua_peach_blossom
tags: Tao_Hua, earthly_branch, relationship, romance, attraction
school: classical
confidence: high

Tao Hua (桃花, Peach Blossom) is the branch that governs personal magnetism and romantic attraction. For Shen-Zi-Chen frame: You is Tao Hua. For Yin-Wu-Xu frame: Mao. For Si-You-Chou frame: Wu. For Hai-Mao-Wei frame: Zi. A natal Peach Blossom in the day or hour pillar enhances personal charm and social magnetism. In a luck or annual cycle, Tao Hua years often bring romance, new social connections, or public recognition. Excess Peach Blossom, especially when clashed, can manifest as scandal or inappropriate relationships.

## Xue_Ren_blood_knife_day
tags: Xue_Ren, day, inauspicious, surgery, injury, earthly_branch
school: classical
confidence: medium

Xue Ren (血刃, Blood Knife) days carry elevated risk of bleeding, injury, or surgical complications. Determined by day branch: Zi and Chou days for Wood Day Masters; Yin and Mao days for Water Day Masters; Chen and Si days for Metal Day Masters; Wu and Wei days for Fire Day Masters; Shen and You days for Earth Day Masters. Classical date selection avoids scheduling surgeries, dental work, or invasive medical procedures on Blood Knife days. The risk is amplified when Xue Ren coincides with a Sui Po or San Sha day.

## Tian_Guan_Fu_Fu_official_seal
tags: day, month, heavenly_stem, auspicious, career, authority, date_selection
school: dong_gong
confidence: medium

Tian Guan Fu (天官符, Heavenly Official Talisman) days are auspicious for activities involving government, official documents, legal proceedings, and career advancement. These days are identified in the traditional almanac by the specific stem-branch combination of the day aligning with the month's official cycle. Submitting applications, attending interviews, or signing contracts with authorities on Tian Guan Fu days increases the likelihood of favorable outcomes. Avoid using these days for contentious legal battles, as the energy amplifies institutional power.
