## Wood_strong_in_spring
tags: Wood, Yin, Mao, spring, season, element, daymaster, strong
school: classical
confidence: high

Wood element reaches peak strength in spring — the months of Yin (Tiger, month 1) and Mao (Rabbit, month 2). Jia and Yi Day Masters born in spring are considered "in season" (得令) and receive natural root support. The chart tends toward strength unless other elements heavily counter. Chen (Dragon, month 3) is a transitional month where Wood begins to weaken and Earth emerges; Wood Day Masters born in Chen must look for additional support in day and hour pillars.

## Fire_strong_in_summer
tags: Fire, Si, Wu, summer, season, element, daymaster, strong
school: classical
confidence: high

Fire element reaches peak strength in summer — the months of Si (Snake, month 4) and Wu (Horse, month 5). Bing and Ding Day Masters born in summer are in season and naturally strong. Wei (Goat, month 6) contains hidden Fire (Ding) alongside Earth and Wood, sustaining Fire into late summer. Summer-born Fire Day Masters often require Water to regulate excess — insufficient Water can manifest as cardiovascular or emotional imbalance. Fire in summer charts also energizes any Wood (Resource) present.

## Metal_strong_in_autumn
tags: Metal, Shen, You, autumn, season, element, daymaster, strong
school: classical
confidence: high

Metal element reaches peak strength in autumn — the months of Shen (Monkey, month 7) and You (Rooster, month 8). Geng and Xin Day Masters born in autumn are in season and carry strong innate power. Xu (Dog, month 9) is a transitional Earth month that stores Metal (Xin) but begins the shift toward winter. Strong Metal without Water expression becomes brittle and unyielding; the most valuable addition to an autumn Metal chart is Water to channel productivity. Metal's season correlates with lung and respiratory system sensitivity.

## Water_strong_in_winter
tags: Water, Hai, Zi, winter, season, element, daymaster, strong
school: classical
confidence: high

Water element reaches peak strength in winter — the months of Hai (Pig, month 10) and Zi (Rat, month 11). Ren and Gui Day Masters born in winter are in season and considered innately strong. Chou (Ox, month 12) stores Water (Gui) alongside Metal and Earth, sustaining Water through late winter. Excessive Water without Earth containment creates flooding — manifesting as scattered thinking, kidney issues, or chaotic energy. Earth and Wood are the most useful additions to balance heavy winter Water charts.

## Earth_strong_in_transitions
tags: Earth, Chen, Wei, Xu, Chou, season, element, daymaster, strong
school: classical
confidence: high

Earth element rules the transitional months between seasons: Chen (Dragon, month 3), Wei (Goat, month 6), Xu (Dog, month 9), and Chou (Ox, month 12). Wu and Ji Day Masters gain moderate strength in any of these months, though less intensely than other elements at their seasonal peak. Earth also maintains underlying strength throughout the year because it grounds all other elements. Wu born in summer (adjacent Fire season) often achieves the strongest Earth by absorbing Fire energy.

## element_out_of_season_weak
tags: daymaster, weak, season, element, resource, companion
school: classical
confidence: high

Any element born out of its governing season is considered "dead" or "resting" (休囚死绝). A Jia Wood Day Master born in autumn (Metal season) loses seasonal support and becomes inherently weak unless strongly rooted in day or hour pillars. Out-of-season Day Masters require strong Resource or Companion support to function. Misreading a weak out-of-season Day Master as strong is a fundamental analytical error.

## seasonal_elemental_cycle_order
tags: Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water, season, element
school: classical
confidence: high

The four seasons follow the elemental productive cycle: Spring (Wood) → Summer (Fire) → Autumn (Metal) → Winter (Water), with Earth governing each transition. Each season nourishes the next — Wood generates Fire, Fire generates Earth, Earth generates Metal, Metal generates Water, Water generates Wood. Understanding which season a chart belongs to reveals the dominant elemental theme and what is naturally supported vs. suppressed at birth.

## seasonal_branch_hidden_stems
tags: earthly_branch, season, element, hidden_stem, Chen, Wei, Xu, Chou
school: classical
confidence: high

Each earthly branch contains hidden stems that reflect its seasonal position. Spring branches (Yin, Mao) primarily store Wood. Summer branches (Si, Wu) store Fire and some Earth. Autumn branches (Shen, You) store Metal. Winter branches (Hai, Zi) store Water. Transitional branches hold multiple elements: Chen stores Yi Wood, Wu Earth, Gui Water; Wei stores Ji Earth, Ding Fire, Yi Wood; Xu stores Xin Metal, Ding Fire, Wu Earth; Chou stores Ji Earth, Gui Water, Xin Metal. These hidden stems activate under clash, combination, or luck cycle triggers.

## spring_born_fire_daymaster
tags: Fire, Yin, Mao, spring, season, element, daymaster, Wood, resource
school: classical
confidence: high

A Fire Day Master (Bing or Ding) born in spring gains strong Resource support from the dominant Wood energy. This is considered a highly favorable configuration — the season nourishes the Day Master through its Resource star. However, if Wood energy is overwhelming without sufficient Fire or Earth, the chart becomes "Resource-heavy" and may produce an academically talented but impractical or indecisive person. Some Fire presence in other pillars is needed to "receive" the Wood resource.

## autumn_born_Wood_daymaster
tags: Wood, Shen, You, autumn, season, element, daymaster, Metal, power
school: classical
confidence: high

A Wood Day Master (Jia or Yi) born in autumn faces the strongest natural opposition — Metal season directly controls Wood. Without compensating support from Water (Resource), Fire (Output), or other Wood (Companion) in the remaining pillars, this is considered a severely challenged chart. The Power star (Metal) bears down on a seasonally weakened Day Master. Autumn-born Wood charts require careful analysis of rootedness in the day and hour branches, as these become the lifeline of the chart.

## seasonal_qi_monthly_influence
tags: season, month, luck_cycle, element, year
school: classical
confidence: medium

The month pillar carries the highest seasonal influence weight in assessing Day Master strength. It represents the dominant qi at birth. The annual pillar (year) provides secondary context. When a luck cycle enters a season sympathetic to the Day Master, the entire decade benefits from the boost — even an out-of-season birth chart can thrive under a favorable seasonal luck cycle. Conversely, entering a hostile seasonal cycle can suppress potential in an otherwise strong chart.

## summer_born_Metal_daymaster
tags: Metal, Si, Wu, summer, season, element, daymaster, Fire, power
school: classical
confidence: high

A Metal Day Master (Geng or Xin) born in summer faces Fire's control at its seasonal peak. Summer Metal is described as "metal in the smelting furnace" — it can be shaped but is under extreme pressure. Whether this is favorable depends on whether additional Water or Earth support is present. With supporting elements, summer Metal charts can represent powerful transformation and refined achievement. Without support, the chart indicates health pressure (lungs, respiratory) and career challenges from authority figures.
