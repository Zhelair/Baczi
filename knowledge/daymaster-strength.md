## strong_daymaster_general
tags: daymaster, strong, resource, companion, output, wealth, power
school: classical
confidence: high

A strong Day Master has abundant self-element support from Resource (both types) and Companion (both types) stars filling multiple pillars, and is born in a favorable season. The strong Day Master benefits from Output, Wealth, and Power stars that channel and deploy excess energy productively. Adding more Resource or Companion to an already strong chart creates imbalance — the Day Master becomes too self-focused, inflexible, or unable to engage with the outer world effectively. Correctly identifying strength is the foundation of BaZi analysis.

## weak_daymaster_general
tags: daymaster, weak, resource, companion, output, wealth, power
school: classical
confidence: high

A weak Day Master lacks sufficient self-element support and is born out of season or in a hostile elemental environment. Weak Day Masters require Resource and Companion stars to function well. Wealth, Output, and Power stars drain the weak Day Master further, creating chronic stress, financial difficulty, and health depletion. A classic error is prescribing Wealth or Power cycles to a weak Day Master because the chart "looks like it should be wealthy or powerful" — without strength, those stars become burdens.

## Jia_Wood_summer_overworked
tags: Jia, Wood, Fire, summer, daymaster, output, resource, Water, Earth
school: classical
confidence: high

Jia (Yang Wood) born in summer is overtaxed by its own productivity — the Fire season draws heavily on the Wood resource. Jia's Output star (Fire) is in season and burns brightly, but this depletes the Jia Day Master who is already weakened by the heat. Water (Resource for Wood) is urgently needed to replenish the Day Master. Earth (Wealth for Wood) can also help by grounding the Fire output into tangible results. Without Water in the chart, a summer Jia tends toward exhaustion despite outward activity.

## Geng_Metal_autumn_needs_water
tags: Geng, Metal, autumn, season, daymaster, Water, output, Earth
school: classical
confidence: high

Geng (Yang Metal) born in autumn is naturally strong — Metal is in season and fully expressed. The most valuable addition for autumn Geng is Water (Output star), which washes and refines the Metal into a productive force. Excessive Earth (Resource for Metal) in an autumn Geng chart creates buried or suffocated Metal — the Day Master becomes heavy and unable to express. Excess Earth without Water is problematic; autumn Geng charts with strong Water output thrive in intellectual, artistic, and fluid-thinking careers.

## Ren_Water_winter_needs_earth
tags: Ren, Water, winter, season, daymaster, Earth, Wood, power, output
school: classical
confidence: high

Ren (Yang Water) born in winter reaches peak power — the chart is dominated by Water energy. Despite this strength, excessive Water without Earth containment creates flooding — directionless, chaotic energy. Earth (Power/Wealth for Water) provides essential containment and structure. Wood Output stars drain the excess Water productively. Without either, winter Ren charts produce individuals with enormous potential that they cannot channel into achievement. Earth in the luck cycle is often the turning point that transforms raw Water power into tangible life success.

## weak_Fire_daymaster_Water_pressure
tags: Fire, Water, daymaster, weak, power, resource, authority
school: classical
confidence: high

A weak Fire Day Master (Bing or Ding) facing strong Water Authority stars experiences extreme pressure without the strength to benefit from it. The Power star (Water) controls rather than elevates the Day Master. Without Resource (Wood) to translate that pressure into nourishment, the weak Fire is simply extinguished. In luck cycles, Water years for weak Fire Day Masters typically bring career setbacks from authority, health issues affecting the heart or eyes, and chronic anxiety. Resource stars (Wood) must be present or arrive in the same cycle to mitigate the damage.
